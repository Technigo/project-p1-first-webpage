PENGUINS

15 Penguin Fun Facts

  1. January 20th is Penguin Awareness Day

  2. Penguins are birds, but they are unable to fly

  3. Penguins do not have teeth. Instead, their tongue and throat have spiny structures that prevent food from slipping out of their mouth.

  4. Penguins can drink salt water because they have special glands that drain the liquid out of the nose and expel the salt.

  5. All penguins spend about 75-80 percent of their life at sea, but they mate and nest on land.

  6. Penguins are serially monogamous; they only have one partner per reproductive season. The emperor penguin is monogamous throughout its life.

  7. In some species, when a male courts a female, it usually offers rocks as a gift.

  8. Penguins are among the 1,500 animal species that practice same-sex coupling.

  9. Male emperor penguins incubate eggs while the female goes hunting.

  10. Five penguin species are classified as endangered: the northern rockhopper penguin, the erect-crested penguin, the yellow-eyed penguin, the African penguin and the Galapagos penguin.

  11. The world's oldest penguin fossil dates back 61 million years.

  12. Some prehistoric penguins were much bigger than the modern ones; they had a height larger than 1.5 meters and weighed about 91 kilograms.

  13. Each penguin has a unique voice, and that’s why they can recognize each other.

  14. Because they aren't used to danger from animals on solid ground, wild penguins exhibit no particular fear of human tourists.

  15. The mandarin word for penguin is 企鹅, which translates roughly to "business goose".
